What is Lexopedia?

Lexopedia is a powerful tool for uncovering lexical similarities between different languages. Our founder created Lexopedia as a personal project to find linguistic connections between as many languages as possible. After realizing its potential, he decided to share it with the world. With Lexopedia, you can discover previously unknown lexical similarities between languages, potentially leading to groundbreaking linguistic breakthroughs. Who knows, you might even be the one to make the next big discovery!

Currently, we're focusing on finding similarities between Nakh languages and Indo-European languages If you find any interesting similarities, please share them with us via Telegram at @chepalgsh26 and we'll publish them on our site.

We're a small team and Lexopedia is a non-profit project. As a student, our founder can't maintain the project without your help. If you enjoy using Lexopedia, please consider donating to our cause at bmc@lexopedia All donations will go towards improving the site and expanding our database of lexical similarities. You can view a list of our generous donors here

In addition to our lexical similarity tool, we also offer dictionaries in various languages. Please bear with us as we're currently working on improving our site. Thank you for using Lexopedia!
